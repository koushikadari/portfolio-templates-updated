About

I'm a backend-leaning software engineer who cares more about what breaks at 3am than what looks good in a demo. Six years in, I've spent most of that time in the unglamorous middle layer of software — queues, caches, service boundaries, the stuff that has to be right for everything above it to feel effortless.

I started out writing Python scripts to automate away a tedious internship task, which turned into a fascination with how systems fail and how to design around that. Today I work mostly in Go and TypeScript, spend a lot of time in AWS, and still enjoy the process of taking a system from "it works on my machine" to "it survived Black Friday."
