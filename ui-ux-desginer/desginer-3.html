Finflow Banking

Client — Finflow Inc.
Category
Web App · Fintech
Industry
Banking & Finance
Duration
14 weeks
Team Size
5 (2 design, 2 eng, 1 PM)

Project Overview

Finflow needed a redesign of their core web banking platform to replace a decade-old interface that was losing customers to more modern challenger banks. The goal was a fast, trustworthy, and accessible experience that made everyday banking feel effortless.

Goals

Reduce time-to-complete for core banking actions, raise accessibility to WCAG AA, and improve trust signals throughout the transfer and statements flows.

Problem Statement

Users struggled to find key actions like transfers and statements, support tickets around navigation had tripled year over year, and the interface failed basic accessibility standards, excluding a meaningful share of older customers.

Research

Conducted 18 user interviews, a competitive audit of 6 challenger banks, and a heuristic evaluation of the existing product. Analytics review revealed a 42% drop-off on the transfer flow specifically.

User Personas

Marta, 34
Busy Professional
Manages finances on mobile between meetings. Wants quick transfers and clear balances without digging through menus.
Carlos, 61
Retiree
Prefers desktop banking, values large legible text and predictable navigation over flashy visuals.

User Journey

01

02
View Dashboard
03
Select Account
04
Initiate Transfer
05
Confirm & Done

Challenges

Legacy backend constraints limited how much the information architecture could change without a full API rebuild, and stakeholders were wary of altering a flow tied to compliance requirements.

Solution

Introduced a simplified three-tab structure, a persistent quick-transfer widget, and a WCAG AA-compliant type and color system, validated through three rounds of usability testing.

Final Outcome

Shipped in phases over one quarter with no disruption to compliance workflows. Customer support tickets related to navigation dropped substantially, and the transfer flow saw a marked lift in completion rate within the first month post-launch.

Orbit SaaS Dashboard

Client — Orbit Analytics
Category
Dashboard · B2B SaaS
Industry
Marketing Technology
Duration
10 weeks
Team Size
4 (1 design, 2 eng, 1 PM)

Project Overview

Orbit Analytics needed a self-serve dashboard that let non-technical marketing teams explore campaign data without waiting on their data team, replacing a static reporting tool exported weekly as spreadsheets.

Goals

Cut reporting turnaround from days to minutes, raise self-serve adoption among non-technical users, and rebuild trust in the underlying data.

Problem Statement

Marketing teams had no way to explore their own data in real time, leading to reporting delays of up to five days and low trust in the numbers by the time they reached decision-makers.

Research

Ran contextual inquiries with 9 marketing managers, mapped existing reporting workflows, and benchmarked against tools like Looker and Mixpanel to identify a genuinely self-serve mental model.

User Personas

Priya, 29
Marketing Manager
Needs weekly campaign snapshots she can build herself and share with leadership without engineering support.
Tom, 41
Head of Growth
Wants a high-level view across campaigns and the ability to drill into anomalies without a data request.

User Journey

01
Connect Data
02
Choose Template
03
Build Widgets
04
Explore & Filter
05
Share Report

Challenges

Balancing flexibility with simplicity was difficult — early prototypes tested as powerful but intimidating for non-technical users, requiring several rounds of simplification.

Solution

Designed a modular widget system with guided templates for common reports, progressive disclosure for advanced filters, and inline explanations of each metric to build trust in the data.

Final Outcome

Launched to all marketing teams with a phased rollout and in-app onboarding. Reporting turnaround dropped from days to minutes, and the dashboard became the team's primary source of truth for campaign performance within the first two months.

Design Process

How I work.

My core process — Discover, Design, Validate — used across every product engagement, from a two-week sprint to a multi-quarter platform redesign.

01

Research Methods

Stakeholder interviews, user interviews, contextual inquiry, competitive audits and analytics review to ground every decision in evidence.

02

User Insights

Synthesizing research into personas, journey maps and jobs-to-be-done to align the team around real user needs before any pixels are drawn.

03

Wireframes

Low-fidelity structure and information architecture, tested early and cheaply before investing in visual design.

04

Prototyping

Interactive, high-fidelity prototypes in Figma or Framer that feel close enough to real to get honest feedback.

05

User Testing

Moderated usability sessions and unmoderated tests to validate flows before they reach engineering.

06

Design Iterations

Rapid revision cycles informed directly by testing data, not opinion — usually 2–3 rounds before final sign-off.

07

Final Solution

A polished, developer-ready design backed by a documented rationale, delivered with specs, assets and a component library.

08

Post-Launch

Monitoring adoption metrics and qualitative feedback after release to guide the next iteration.
